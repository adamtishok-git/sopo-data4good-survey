About This Survey & Data
Our Intent
This community-led project was developed to capture the perspectives of parents and educators regarding the upcoming school reconfiguration. As the district navigates this transition, our goal is to identify the most critical gaps and essential supports from those directly impacted. By highlighting what matters most to families and staff, we aim to help the school department focus its planning on the highest-priority areas.
Informing Readiness and Transparency
We believe that the expert opinions of district educators and staff are vital to understanding the operational and educational needs of our schools. This survey is designed to inform the readiness and timing of the transition while providing the School Board, the District, and the community with transparent data on collective stakeholder sentiment.
Methodology & Resource Allocation
With the District currently facing an $8M budget deficit and a 12% reduction in staffing, the allocation of finite resources is a significant challenge. To ensure the most essential needs are clearly identified, we utilized a forced-choice methodology. This requires respondents to prioritize specific outcomes, providing a clearer roadmap of "must-haves" versus "nice-to-haves" in a resource-constrained environment.
Important Disclosures
Independent & Community-Led: This initiative was created and led by community members independently of the School Board or District leadership. However, educators within the District were consulted during the development of the form to ensure questions were relevant and grounded in the classroom experience.
Non-Scientific Data: This survey was conducted using Google Forms without authentication. As a non-scientific, anonymous survey, the results should be viewed as a "pulse check" of the community rather than a controlled statistical study.
Data Usage: All data collected is anonymous and is intended to assist in transition planning. No individual names or identifying information were collected.
